# AI Knowledge NAS — Business Context

## Product family

Part of [[neural-foundry|Neural Foundry]]. Primary product modules: [[neural-vault|Neural Vault]], [[neural-forge|Neural Forge]], [[neural-lens|Neural Lens]].

## Business Goals

- Turn unmanaged organizational data into queryable, agent-usable knowledge.
- Provide an on-prem/private alternative to cloud-only enterprise AI knowledge tools.
- Create a reusable infrastructure component for OSForge/OSFoundry and client deployments.
- Enable AI agents to reason over client file systems, project docs, media, SaaS systems, and databases without bespoke integrations per project.

## Stakeholders

- Primary sponsor: Nick / TIC Solutions Corp / Neural Industries.
- Primary users: business teams, technical teams, AI agents, project managers, analysts.
- Secondary users: IT/security admins, compliance teams, consultants, client stakeholders.

## Market Context

Relevant markets overlap:

- Enterprise search / knowledge management.
- NAS/object storage and document management.
- RAG/GraphRAG platforms.
- AI data connectors and ETL.
- eDiscovery/compliance search.
- Agent infrastructure / memory systems.

Differentiation thesis: most products are either storage-first, search-first, or RAG-first. This concept is agent-first and storage-aware: a durable knowledge substrate that understands files and exposes both human and machine interfaces.

## Strategic Alignment

- Strong fit with OSForge and OSFoundry.
- Could become the knowledge substrate for Prism, Watchtower, AgentOS registry, and future client deployments.
- Fits DGX Spark/on-prem strategy: local extraction and embeddings preserve privacy while cloud frontier models remain optional.

## Potential Commercial Forms

- Self-hosted enterprise appliance/software.
- Managed cloud deployment in Azure/AWS.
- Internal OSForge platform component.
- Project-specific client deployment paired with Prism/OSFoundry.

## Success Metrics

- Time from file added to searchable/answerable knowledge.
- Retrieval precision/recall and citation quality.
- Number of supported file/source types.
- Agent task success using CLI/API knowledge access.
- Cost per indexed GB/TB.
- Query latency for chat/search/API.
- Admin trust: permissions respected, provenance clear, audit logs complete.
